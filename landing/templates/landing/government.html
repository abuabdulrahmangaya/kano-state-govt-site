Executive

Governor

Abba Kabir Yusuf is a Nigerian politician currently serving as the governor of Kano State since assuming office in 2023. Born on January 5, 1963, in Gaya Local Government Area of Kano State, Yusuf received his early Islamic education under the guidance of his late grandfather, Alhaji Yusuf Bashari, who was the District Head of Gaya.

Yusuf attended Sumaila Primary School from 1968 to 1975 and later completed his secondary education at Government Secondary School Dawakin Tofa and Government Secondary School Lautai in Gumel in 1980. He pursued further studies at the Federal Polytechnic, Mubi, earning a National Diploma (ND) in Civil Engineering in 1985. He then obtained a Higher National Diploma (HND) in Civil Engineering with specialization in Water Resources/Environmental Engineering from Kaduna Polytechnic in 1989. Additionally, Yusuf pursued postgraduate studies at Bayero University, Kano, earning a postgraduate diploma and a master's degree in business administration.

In 1989, Yusuf served the mandatory National Youth Service Corps (NYSC) at the Kaduna Environmental Protection Agency (KEPA). He subsequently joined the Kano State Water Resources Engineering and Construction Agency (WRECA), holding various positions.

Yusuf began his political career as personal assistant to Rabiu Kwankwaso during Kwankwaso's tenure as governor of Kano State and continued in that role when Kwankwaso became a federal minister in 2003. In 2011, he was appointed Commissioner of Works, Housing, and Transport during Kwankwaso's second term as governor.

From 2009 to 2011, Yusuf also served as chairman of the Governing Board of the National Institute for Educational Planning and Administration (NIEPA) in Ondo State, appointed by President Umaru Musa Yaradua.

In 2018, Yusuf contested the Kano State gubernatorial election under the platform of the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) but lost to the incumbent Governor Abdullahi Umar Ganduje. He later contested the results in court.

In 2022, Yusuf joined the New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP) and was endorsed by Rabiu Kwankwaso to contest again in the 2023 elections. He emerged victorious on March 20, 2023, and was inaugurated alongside his running mate, Aminu Abdussalam Gwarzo, on May 29, 2023, as the governor of Kano State.

Deputy Governor

Aminu Abdussalam Gwarzo, born on November 6, 1960, is a Nigerian politician currently serving as the Deputy Governor of Kano State. He hails from Gwarzo Local Government Area in Kano State.

Gwarzo's educational journey includes attending Gwarzo Primary School from 1966 to 1972, followed by his secondary education from 1972 to 1977. He furthered his studies at Kano State Polytechnic, where he obtained both a National Diploma and a Higher National Diploma. Additionally, he pursued a Postgraduate Diploma in Public Policy and Administration at Bayero University, Kano, from 2007 to 2008.

Beginning his career as a classroom teacher in 1977, Gwarzo later became a headmaster in 1981. His professional path transitioned into public service as a revenue officer after completing his studies in Banking and Finance and Accountancy. He served in this capacity for six years before entering politics, where he was elected as the Chairman of Gwarzo Local Government in 1996.

Gwarzo's political career continued to ascend, and he became the Commissioner for State Affairs from 2011 to 2015 during Governor Rabiu Kwankwaso's administration. He has also held the position of Chairman of Gwarzo Local Government Area twice, first in 1996 and subsequently from 1999 to 2003 under the Peoples Democratic Party (PDP) while Kwankwaso was the governor of Kano State.

In March 2023, Gwarzo was elected as the Deputy Governor of Kano State under the platform of the New Nigeria Peoples Party (NNPP), alongside Abba Kabir Yusuf, who was elected as the governor. Notably, Gwarzo had previously served as Yusuf's running mate since the 2019 election, demonstrating a longstanding commitment to public service and governance in Kano State.

History of Kano Emirate

The Hausa Kingdom of Kano was based on an ancient settlement of Dala Hill. While small chiefdoms were previously present in the area, according to the Kano Chronicle, Bagauda, a grandson of the mythical hero Bayajidda,[2] became the first king of Kano in 999, reigning until 1063. Muhammad Rumfa ascended to the throne in 1463 and reigned until 1499. During his reign he reformed the city, expanded the Sahelian Gidan Rumfa (Emir's Palace), and played a role in the further Islamization of the city as he urged prominent residents to convert.[5][6] The Hausa state remained independent until the Fulani conquest of 1805.

At the beginning of the 19th century, Kano became the largest and most prosperous province of the empire.[7] It was one of the last major slave societies.

From 1893 until 1895, two rival claimants for the throne fought a civil war. With the help of royal slaves, Yusufu was victorious over Tukur and claimed the title of emir.

Kano has long been renowned as a major trading hub in west Africa. Situated on important trans-Saharan trade routes, the city facilitated the exchange of goods.
